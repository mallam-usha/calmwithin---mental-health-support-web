Box Breathing

A simple technique used by Navy SEALs to stay calm and focused in stressful situations.

4-4-4-4
Breathe In

How to practice Box Breathing:

  1. Inhale deeply through your nose for 4 seconds
  2. Hold your breath for 4 seconds
  3. Exhale slowly through your mouth for 4 seconds
  4. Hold your breath for 4 seconds
  5. Repeat the cycle for 3-5 minutes

Benefits:

  • Reduces stress and anxiety quickly
  • Improves focus and concentration
  • Helps regulate the nervous system
  • Can be done anywhere, anytime

4-7-8 Breathing

Developed by Dr. Andrew Weil, this technique promotes relaxation and better sleep.

4-7-8
Breathe In

How to practice 4-7-8 Breathing:

  1. Place the tip of your tongue behind your upper front teeth
  2. Exhale completely through your mouth
  3. Close your mouth and inhale quietly through your nose for 4 seconds
  4. Hold your breath for 7 seconds
  5. Exhale completely through your mouth for 8 seconds
  6. Repeat the cycle 3-4 times

Benefits:

  • Promotes relaxation and reduces anxiety
  • Helps with insomnia and sleep difficulties
  • Can help manage cravings and emotional responses
  • Lowers heart rate and blood pressure

Diaphragmatic Breathing

Also known as belly breathing, this technique engages the diaphragm for deeper relaxation.

Inhale-Exhale
Breathe In

How to practice Diaphragmatic Breathing:

  1. Lie on your back or sit comfortably with one hand on your chest and one on your belly
  2. Inhale slowly through your nose for 4-5 seconds, feeling your belly rise
  3. Keep your chest relatively still
  4. Exhale slowly through pursed lips for 6-7 seconds, feeling your belly fall
  5. Repeat for 5-10 minutes

Benefits:

  • Reduces the body's stress response
  • Improves oxygen exchange
  • Lowers heart rate and blood pressure
  • Helps manage symptoms of depression and anxiety disorders
  • Strengthens the diaphragm
